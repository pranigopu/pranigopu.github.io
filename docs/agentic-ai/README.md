# About
The evolution of AI from specialised ML to more general generative AI (including LLMs, diffusion models, generative adversarial networks, etc.) has opened up a key cognitive capability: adaptive, non-procedural reasoning. This, in turn, has led to the realisation of the vision of an AI agent, i.e. an AI-powered system that can act and interact as an autonomous agent in contexts requiring such autonomy in decision-making (e.g. regular data-quality checks, adaptive IoT systems (especially in industrial contexts), regular natural-language request handlers (e.g. customer assistants, customer ticket handlers, etc.), meeting transcript summarisation, etc.).

This potential seems immense in theory, but what are the implications of this potential in practice? I.e. what does this capacity of AI-powered agents to act autonomously mean for practical contexts, in terms of safety, reliability, effectiveness and efficiency? How would they work, and would this way of working enable us to establish robust, reliable solutions, or create new complications, or both? What is an agentic AI system anyway, how can it be effectively implemented, and who, in the end of the day, needs it at all? These are the questions I shall aim to answer.
